Writing Guidance

Personal Statement Advice

Structural Advice

Follow the recommended 47-line (4000 char) UCAS structure.

Paragraph 1: Introduction & Hook (10%)

Explain why you want to study this course. Focus on a specific academic concept, book, or lecture that triggered your passion. Avoid clichés like "Since I was young..." or quotes.

Paragraph 2 & 3: Supercurricular Exploration (70%-80%)

This is the core of your statement. Detail your readings, independent research, online courses, and relevant summer schools. Use the ABC method:
• Activity: What you did (e.g., read a paper).
• Benefit: What you learned/thought about it.
• Course connection: How it prepares you for your degree.

Paragraph 4: Extracurriculars & Skills (10%-15%)

Briefly mention hobbies, sports, part-time jobs, or school leadership roles. Emphasize transferable skills like time management, leadership, or teamwork, and connect them back to university life.

Paragraph 5: Conclusion (5%)

A concise summary of your suitability and passion. Reiterate why you will excel on the course and how it aligns with your future ambitions.
